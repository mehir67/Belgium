4. Chocolate & desserts

Belgium is world-famous for chocolate:

  • Pralines (filled chocolates) were invented here

  • Famous chocolatiers: Neuhaus, Godiva, Leonidas, Pierre Marcolini
    Other sweets include speculoos biscuits, tarte au riz, and cuberdons (cone-shaped candies).

5. Beer culture (very important)

Belgium treats beer like wine:

  • Over 1,500 varieties

  • Famous styles: Trappist, Abbey, Lambic, Gueuze, Kriek

  • Each beer has its own specific glass
    Beer is also used in cooking, not just drinking.

 

 

6. Regional differences

  • Flanders (north): richer, beer-based dishes

  • Wallonia (south): more rustic, meat-heavy cuisine

  • Brussels: international + traditional Belgian mix

7. High-end gastronomy

Belgium punches above its weight in Michelin-starred restaurants. Chefs focus on:

  • High-quality local ingredients

  • Precise technique

  • Generous portions (very Belgian)

8. Food culture vibe

Belgians take food seriously but aren’t pretentious:

  • Quality over speed

  • Long meals, good conversation

  • Simple dishes done really well
